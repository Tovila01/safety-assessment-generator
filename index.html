    Risk Rating Method

    Reference explanation for how a chemical risk rating can be assessed.

    Open Risk Rating Explanation Reference method and action bands

    1. Identify the Hazards

    Most workplaces start from standardized hazard information such as GHS / CLP or NFPA-style hazard levels.

    • 0: Minimal hazard
    • 1: Slight hazard
    • 2: Moderate hazard
    • 3: Serious hazard
    • 4: Extreme or severe danger

    2. Assess Severity

    • Low: Minor injury or reversible health effects
    • Medium: Medical treatment required
    • High: Serious injury, long-term health effects, or death

    3. Assess Probability

    • Unlikely: Adverse event occurs only rarely
    • Likely: Event occurs on a frequent basis
    • Highly likely: Adverse event is almost certain to occur
    Risk level Matrix score Action required
    Low 1 to 2 Acceptable. Maintain existing controls. No further action normally needed.
    Medium 3 to 6 Tolerable. Review whether extra controls such as ventilation or PPE are justified.
    High 8 to 16 Unacceptable. Stop the process until engineering and safety controls are in place.

    Current app behavior: this tool still uses a simplified internal worst-case method based on extracted H-codes. It does not yet calculate a full exposure-based probability × severity matrix automatically.
